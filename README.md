# Dream Machines: The History of Artificial Imagination

## Project Overview

Dream Machines is a museum-style website that explores how humans imagined intelligent machines before they fully existed. The site follows the history of artificial imagination through myth, mechanical automata, early computing, artificial intelligence, and generative technology.

The goal of this project is to make the website feel like a curated digital exhibition instead of a basic class website. Each section is designed like a museum wing, guiding the visitor through a larger story about how human dreams, fears, inventions, and creative ideas shaped the development of machine intelligence.

## Museum Concept

The website is organized as a digital museum with three main exhibition wings:

- Mythic Hall
- Mechanical Wing
- Digital Vault

Each wing focuses on a different stage in the history of artificial imagination. The visitor begins with myths and legendary artificial beings, then moves into mechanical inventions and programmable machines, and finally reaches modern artificial intelligence and generative systems.

## Design Style

I used a museum editorial / archival exhibition design style.

This style uses large serif typography, soft paper-like background colors, quiet spacing, exhibit labels, and structured sections. The design is meant to feel like a professional museum catalogue or curated exhibition page.

The visual style supports the topic because the website is not only about technology. It is also about history, imagination, culture, and human ideas. The calm layout and museum-like structure help the visitor feel like they are moving through an educational exhibition.

## Brand Archetype

I chose the Sage brand archetype.

The Sage archetype fits this project because the website is focused on knowledge, history, learning, and explanation. The site acts like a guide that helps visitors understand where artificial intelligence came from and why it matters.

This archetype influenced the tone of the website. The writing is thoughtful, educational, and reflective instead of casual or entertainment-focused.

## Method of Persuasion

I used the Cialdini principle of Authority.

The site uses authority by presenting the content in the style of a curated museum exhibition. The organized structure, historical timeline, exhibit labels, and educational tone make the website feel more trustworthy and serious.

This encourages visitors to spend more time exploring the site because the information feels carefully selected and professionally presented.

I also used curiosity as a secondary engagement method. The three wings encourage visitors to keep moving through the exhibition to see how the story develops from myth to machinery to modern AI.

## AI Orchestration Process

For this project, I used AI as part of a structured workflow instead of asking for one final answer in a single step.

My process followed this pattern:

Spec → QA → Sprint → QA → Implement → QA

First, I defined the main concept of the museum website. Then I reviewed the idea and selected the design style, persuasion principle, and brand archetype. After that, I broke the project into smaller parts, including the homepage, navigation, exhibit wings, timeline, and individual content sections.

I used AI to help plan the structure, improve the writing, review the design, and identify problems in the visitor experience. After each change, I reviewed the site again and made improvements based on what was not working.

This process helped me stay organized, reduce drift, and keep the site aligned with the project goals.

## Curator Review Approach

I reviewed the website as if it were a curated exhibition instead of just a normal website.

The review focused on:

- narrative flow
- exhibit organization
- artifact presentation
- educational value
- emotional engagement
- visual coherence
- visitor experience

This helped me improve the project so that it feels more like a museum experience. The site now has clearer sections, stronger navigation, more consistent visual language, and a better connection between the topic and the design.

## Research Notes

Before building the final version of the site, I researched the major stages in the history of artificial imagination. I focused on moments where humans imagined, designed, or built artificial beings and intelligent systems.

The research helped shape the three main museum wings:

- Mythic Hall focuses on early stories of artificial life, including Talos, golems, and legendary made beings.
- Mechanical Wing focuses on mechanical automata, Leonardo’s mechanical knight, Babbage’s engines, Ada Lovelace, and the idea of programmable procedure.
- Digital Vault focuses on Alan Turing, machine intelligence, machine learning, neural networks, and generative AI.

This research helped me avoid making the site a random collection of pages. Instead, the site follows a guided route from myth to machinery to modern artificial intelligence.

## Final Review Improvements

After the midterm review, I improved the project by:

- changing the site from a basic dark layout into a more professional museum editorial style
- creating separate exhibit pages for Mythic Hall, Mechanical Wing, and Digital Vault
- improving the navigation so visitors can move through the museum clearly
- fixing layout problems on the chapter pages
- improving the README to explain the design style, archetype, persuasion principle, and AI process more clearly
- making the live site available through GitHub Pages

These improvements helped the site feel more like a guided exhibition instead of a simple class website.
